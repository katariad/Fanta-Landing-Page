About Fanta

Fanta is a brand of fruit-flavored carbonated soft drinks created by The Coca-Cola Company. The original Fanta drink was created in Germany in 1940, during World War II, when the Coca-Cola syrup could not be imported into the country due to trade restrictions. As a result, the head of Coca-Cola Deutschland, Max Keith, decided to create a new product using available ingredients. The name "Fanta" was derived from the German word "Fantasie," which means "imagination" or "fantasy." The original Fanta flavor was made from whey and apple pomace, resulting in a fruity taste. After the war, Fanta was introduced to other countries and underwent various flavor changes and adaptations to suit local tastes. Today, Fanta is available in a wide range of flavors worldwide, including orange, grape, strawberry, pineapple, and many others. It is known for its vibrant colors and fruity taste, making it a popular choice among consumers of all ages.
